Consumption and Emissions (Consume) Output Parameters

Consumption

Name Units Brief Description
Canopy Consumption Tons per acre, kg per meter squared Consumption of the fuel layer that characterizes the fuels in the tree (branches and foliage only), snag, and ladder fuel categories of the canopy stratum. Live tree bole wood is not included in canopy fuels because bole wood is not considered available to burn.
Ground Fuels Consumption Tons per acre, kg per meter squared Consumption of the fuel stratum that characterizes the duff, squirrel midden, and basal accumulation fuel categories.  Basal accumulation is bark, downed woody debris, litter, and duff surrounding tree trunks.
Litter-Lichen-Moss Consumption Tons per acre, kg per meter squared Consumption of the top layer of the forest or rangeland floor composed of loose debris of dead sticks, branches, twigs, dead grass, and recently fallen leaves or needles, live moss, and live and dead lichen.
Non-woody Consumption Tons per acre, kg per meter squared Consumption of the fuel layer that characterizes the grass, sedge, and other herbaceous fuels.  Non-woody fuels lack a true woody stem; this is what distinguishes them from woody fuels.
Shrub Consumption Tons per acre, kg per meter squared Consumption of the fuel layer that characterizes the shrub fuels. Shrubs are woody perennial plants that differ from trees because of their low stature and because they generally produce several basal stems instead of a single bole.
Total Consumption Tons per acre, kg per meter squared Calculated total fuel consumption of all available fuel (live and dead) contained within the area under study.
Woody Consumption Tons per acre, kg per meter squared Consumption of all woody fuels. Woody fuels include sound wood, rotten wood, and stumps.
