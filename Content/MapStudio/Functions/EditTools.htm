Edit/Create Shapes

Edit/Create Shapes allows you to create, edit, and save polygons (shapefiles) in IFTDSS, that can be used as areas of interest or landscape masks.

Save a polygon(s) you just uploaded to Map Studio

Currently, this extra step is required to save an uploaded shapefile in My Workspace for later use in IFTDSS. Without this step, navigating away from Map Studio results in an uploaded shapefile not being saved. This is a known issue that will be corrected in a later IFTDSS release.

To retain your uploaded shapefiles in IFTDSS if you follow the steps below:

Upload a zipped shapefile, ensure you select which attributes you would like to include (you may choose up to 10).

When completed, a confirmation message will appear to verify the shapefile has been uploaded

To select the new shapefile, exit Add Shapefile and open Layer List to zoom to your new shapefile, by

  1. selecting the icon to the right of your shapefile and
  2. selecting the Zoom to option

Open Edit Tools and select the Copy from Existing Features button

Select the polygon you wish to save (selected polygons will turn yellow). This can be done by either selecting individual polygons or selecting and dragging a box over the polygons you wish to retain.

if you plan on selecting multiple polygons, select the Select mulitpart option under the copy from Existing Features button .

Left click any remaining polygons you wish to save as part of your retained shapefile.

Currently you must select Copy from Existing Features button before highlighting each additional polygon.

Once all polygons you wish to retain have been selected, enter a name for your shapefile and choose a folder location for the polygons to be saved. Saved polygons will then be available in My Workspace and viewable in Map Studio for future use throughout IFTDSS.

Draw a Polygon in Map Studio with the extent, polygon, or freehand button and save.

Drag a Rectangle: To drag a rectangular extent, select the Extent button , navigate to the portion of the map where you intend to place the corner of the rectangle. Hold down the left mouse button, drag the cursor until you have your desired area highlighted in yellow, and release the left mouse button. To discard your polygon, use the Clear button. To edit your polygon, double left click anywhere within the area of the polygon. To save, give the polygon a name, specify the folder in which you would like to store the polygon, and select Create.

Snap Points: To snap a polygon using points, select the Polygon button , navigate to the portion of the map where you intend to place your polygon, and single left click the mouse to place your first point. Go the location where you want your second point, single left click again, and continue this process until you have drawn you polygon. Double left click to finish. To discard your polygon, use the Clear button. To edit your polygon, double left click anywhere within the area of the polygon. To save, give the polygon a name, specify the folder in which you would like to store the polygon, and select Create.

Draw Freehand: To draw a polygon freehand, select the Freehand button , navigate to the portion of the map where you intend to place your polygon, and hold down the left mouse key. While continuing to hold down the left mouse key, use the mouse to draw your polygon, and when you are finished, release the left mouse button. To discard your polygon, use the Clear button. To edit your polygon, double left click anywhere within the area of the polygon. To save, give the polygon a name, specify the folder in which you would like to store the polygon, and select Create.

Copy and Edit A Polygon in Map Studio

Open Edit Tools from the bottom of Map Studio and Select Copy from Existing Features. To only edit one polygon choose Select Singlepart, or edit multiple polygons choose Select multipart. Scroll to the map and double left click the mouse button over the polygon you intend to edit, this will reveal the polygon vertices and an editing box

Move a vertex by hovering over it, holding down the left mouse key, and dragging it to the correct position.

Delete a vertex by hovering over it, clicking the right mouse button once, and selecting delete

Continue your edits until you are satisfied with your shapefile.

When you are finished, save the corrections as a new shapefile by giving it a descriptive name in Edit Tools, specify the folder to save the file to, and select Create. The new shapefile will now be available in My Workspace for use throughout IFTDSS. NOTE THIS FEATURE IS NOT WORKING CURRENTLY 2-28-17-JH

Trying to find a polygon on a landscape where you have many polygons displayed at once? Try filtering using the Attribute Table.

Any polygons saved with the Edit Tool will be permanently saved to the IFTDSS system and will be available in MyWorkspace, and the generate, edit, and summarize landscape interfaces.
